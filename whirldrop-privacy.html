Privacy Policy for Whirldrop

Last updated: 4 June 2026

Whirldrop is a casual board game (a drop-and-connect game with rotating gravity). We keep data to the minimum needed to run online play and rankings.

No email or personal identity required. No in-app purchases. We show ads via Google AdMob (see section 6).

1. What We Store

On your device (offline modes): your settings (sound, haptics, theme, language, board rules) and local play statistics (games, wins, draws, points). This never leaves your device for local or play-vs-computer games.

In the cloud (only when you use online features): to enable online multiplayer and rankings we use Google Firebase (Anonymous Authentication + Cloud Firestore). The first time you use an online/account feature we create an anonymous account — a random identifier with no email, name, or personal information. Linked to that identifier we store:

  • a username you choose (a display name shown to opponents and on rankings — please don't use your real name or any personal information);
  • your ranking points and win/draw counts;
  • while an online match is live: the match's rules and the moves played (column numbers), plus a periodic "still connected" timestamp used to detect when a player leaves or loses connection;
  • optional email sign-in: if you choose to sign in with an email and password (purely to save and restore your progress across devices), we store your email address linked to the account (the password is handled by Firebase Authentication; we never see or store it). This is entirely optional — the game works fully without it.

This data is processed on Google's servers under Google's terms. We do not sell data or share it with advertisers.

2. What We Do Not Collect

No phone number, real name, contacts, location, or photos. We don't require an email — one is stored only if you opt in to email sign-in (section 1). We run no analytics of our own. (Our ad provider, Google AdMob, may access a device advertising identifier to serve and measure ads — see section 6.)

3. Permissions

The app uses Internet access only for the optional online features above. Two players on one device and play-vs-computer work fully offline.

4. Children's Privacy

Whirldrop does not knowingly collect personal information from children. The only user-provided data is a freely chosen, non-personal username. There are no in-app purchases. The app shows ads via Google AdMob (section 6).

5. Data Deletion

You can delete your account and its cloud data at any time, directly in the app: Profile → Delete account. This permanently removes your profile (username, ranking points, win/draw counts) and your leaderboard entry, and deletes the underlying authentication account (including a linked email, if any). The action is immediate and cannot be undone. Alternatively, you may request deletion by contacting us (section 8) with your in-app username and approximate creation date.

6. Advertising

Whirldrop shows ads through Google AdMob: a full-screen (interstitial) ad after some online games, and an optional rewarded ad you can choose to watch to earn points. AdMob is a Google service that may collect and use a device advertising identifier and related data to serve and measure ads, under Google's policies. In regions that require it (e.g. the EEA/UK) we show a consent prompt before any personalised ads. You can reset or limit the advertising identifier in your device settings.

7. Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new "Last updated" date.
